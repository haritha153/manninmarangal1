Gulmohar Tree

Delonix regia

The Royal Flame of Summer Gardens

Basic Information

The Gulmohar, also known as the Royal Poinciana or Flame Tree, is one of the most spectacular flowering trees in the world. Native to Madagascar, it has become a beloved ornamental tree across tropical and subtropical regions. With its brilliant orange-red flowers and feathery foliage, the Gulmohar transforms landscapes into fiery displays during the summer months.

Scientific Classification

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Delonix
  • Species: D. regia

Names in Different Languages

  • Tamil: மயிலால் (Mayilaal)
  • Hindi: गुलमोहर (Gulmohar)
  • Malayalam: കൃഷ്ണചൂര (Krishnachoora)
  • Telugu: తుర్రాయి (Turrayi)
  • Marathi: गुलमोहर (Gulmohar)

Physical Characteristics

  • Height: 10–15 meters at maturity
  • Leaves: Bipinnate, feathery, fern-like
  • Flowers: Orange-red with scarlet markings
  • Blooming: April to June (summer season)

Medicinal & Cultural Significance

Medicinal Uses

  • Bark extract used for treating malaria and fever
  • Flowers have anti-diabetic properties
  • Leaves used in traditional wound healing
  • Seeds contain compounds with antimicrobial effects

Cultural Importance

  • Symbol of elegance and flamboyant beauty
  • Popular choice for avenue planting and parks
  • Featured in poetry and literature
  • Associated with summer festivals in India

Environmental Benefits

  • Excellent shade provider during hot summers
  • Attracts butterflies, bees, and hummingbirds
  • Helps reduce urban heat island effect
  • Natural air purifier in urban environments

Cultivation & Care

Sunlight

Gulmohar trees require full sunlight exposure for at least 6-8 hours daily. They bloom most profusely in bright, sunny locations.

Watering

Moderate watering is needed. Young trees require regular watering, while mature trees are quite drought-tolerant.

Soil

Adaptable to various soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy loam. Can tolerate slightly alkaline to neutral pH levels.

Pruning

Prune after flowering season to maintain shape. Remove dead branches and suckers. Light pruning encourages better flowering.

Interesting Facts

Spectacular Blooming

During peak flowering, the entire canopy is covered with brilliant orange-red flowers, creating one of nature's most stunning displays.

Umbrella Crown

Mature trees develop a distinctive flat-topped, umbrella-shaped crown that provides excellent shade coverage.

Seed Pods

Produces large, flat, dark brown seed pods up to 60cm long that remain on the tree for months after flowering.

Global Favorite

Despite being native to Madagascar, it's now planted worldwide in tropical regions and is considered an iconic tropical tree.

Seasonal Characteristics

Spring (March-May)

New leaves emerge as the tree prepares for the flowering season. The canopy becomes lush and green.

Summer (June-August)

Peak flowering period with masses of orange-red blooms. The tree becomes a spectacular focal point in landscapes.

Monsoon (September-November)

Seed pods develop and mature. The tree maintains its green foliage with occasional late blooms.

Winter (December-February)

Tree becomes semi-deciduous, shedding some leaves. This is the dormant period before the next flowering cycle.
