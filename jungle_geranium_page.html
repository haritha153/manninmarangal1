Jungle Geranium

Ixora coccinea

The Colorful Cluster of Tropical Gardens

Basic Information

The Jungle Geranium, scientifically known as Ixora coccinea, is a vibrant flowering shrub native to Southern India and Sri Lanka. Despite its common name, it is not related to true geraniums but belongs to the coffee family. Known for its dense clusters of small, tubular flowers in brilliant colors, it is a popular choice for tropical gardens and landscaping.

Scientific Classification

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Ixora
  • Species: I. coccinea

Physical Characteristics

  • Height: 1–3 meters as a shrub
  • Leaves: Glossy, dark green, opposite
  • Flowers: Small, tubular, in dense clusters
  • Blooming: Year-round in tropical climates

Medicinal & Cultural Significance

Medicinal Uses

  • Leaves used to treat wounds and skin infections
  • Root decoction for treating diarrhea
  • Flowers used in traditional eye treatments
  • Anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties

Cultural Importance

  • Sacred flower in Hindu religious ceremonies
  • Used in temple decorations and garlands
  • Symbol of love and devotion in South Indian culture
  • Featured in traditional wedding decorations

Ornamental Value

  • Popular hedge plant and border shrub
  • Excellent for container gardening
  • Attracts butterflies and hummingbirds
  • Available in multiple color varieties

Cultivation & Care

Sunlight

Jungle Geranium thrives in bright, indirect light to partial shade. Too much direct sunlight can scorch the leaves and flowers.

Watering

Requires consistent moisture but not waterlogged soil. Water regularly during growing season, reduce in winter months.

Soil

Prefers well-drained, slightly acidic to neutral soil rich in organic matter. Good drainage is essential to prevent root rot.

Pruning

Regular pruning after flowering promotes bushier growth and more blooms. Pinch growing tips to encourage branching.

Flower Colors & Varieties

Red Varieties

The classic coral-red or scarlet flowers are the most common and traditional variety, creating vibrant clusters.

Pink Varieties

Soft pink to deep rose-colored blooms offer a more subtle beauty while maintaining the characteristic cluster formation.

Yellow Varieties

Bright yellow flowers provide a cheerful alternative, often called 'Ixora lutea' or Golden Ixora.

White Varieties

Pure white flowers create elegant displays, perfect for moon gardens and formal landscape designs.

Growing Tips & Benefits

Indoor Growing

Can be grown as a houseplant in bright, humid conditions. Requires regular misting and consistent temperature.

Fertilization

Benefits from regular feeding with balanced fertilizer during growing season. Avoid over-fertilization which can reduce flowering.

Pest Management

Generally pest-resistant but may attract aphids and scale insects. Good air circulation helps prevent fungal issues.

Companion Planting

Pairs well with ferns, caladiums, and other shade-loving tropical plants in mixed border plantings.
