Marigold

Tagetes

A vibrant flower widely used in decorations and ceremonies

Basic Information

Marigolds are annual or perennial herbaceous plants in the genus Tagetes. Native to the Americas, several species are widely cultivated and have become naturalized worldwide, including India, where they are very common and hold cultural significance.

Scientific Classification

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Angiosperms
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Asterales
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Tagetes

Names in Different Languages

  • English: Marigold
  • Hindi: गेंदा (Genda)
  • Tamil: செவ்வந்தி (Sevvandhi)
  • Bengali: গাঁদা (Gঁada)
  • Telugu: బంతి పువ్వు (Banti Puvvu)

Physical Characteristics

  • Height: Typically 0.1 to 2.2 meters (4 inches to 7 feet) depending on the species.
  • Flowers: Showy, often yellow, orange, red, or a combination, with ray and disc florets.
  • Leaves: Pinnately compound or deeply lobed, usually aromatic.
  • Scent: Often has a strong, sometimes pungent odor.
  • Blooming Season: Varies by species but often blooms from summer to frost.

Cultural Significance in India

Marigolds hold a special place in Indian culture and traditions:

Decorations and Festivals

Marigold garlands and loose flowers are extensively used for decorations during festivals, weddings, and religious ceremonies due to their vibrant colors and auspicious connotations.

Religious Offerings

Marigolds are commonly offered to deities in temples and during prayers.

Traditional Medicine

Some species of marigold have traditional medicinal uses, including antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Uses & Benefits

Marigolds are valued for various reasons:

Ornamental Plant

Widely cultivated for their attractive and long-lasting flowers in gardens and landscapes.

Natural Dye

The flowers can be used to extract natural yellow and orange dyes for fabrics and food coloring.

Pest Repellent

Some marigold species are known to repel certain garden pests, making them useful companion plants.

Interesting Facts

Diversity

There are over 50 species of marigolds in the genus Tagetes.

Easy to Grow

Marigolds are relatively easy to grow and are popular among home gardeners.

Edible Flowers

Some marigold petals are edible and can be used in salads and as a garnish.
