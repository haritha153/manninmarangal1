Yellow Flame Tree

Peltophorum pterocarpum

The Golden Spectacle of Tropical Landscapes

Basic Information

The Yellow Flame Tree is a spectacular deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia and Northern Australia. Known for its brilliant golden-yellow flowers that create a stunning canopy, it is widely planted as an ornamental street tree and shade provider. The tree gets its name from the flame-like appearance of its dense clusters of bright yellow blooms.

Scientific Classification

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Peltophorum
  • Species: P. pterocarpum

Names in Different Languages

  • Tamil: இராஜ மரம் (Raja Maram)
  • Hindi: गुलमोहर पीला (Gulmohar Peela)
  • Malayalam: കൊങ്കണി (Konkani)
  • Telugu: సీమ చింత (Seema Chinta)
  • English: Copper Pod, Golden Flamboyant

Physical Characteristics

  • Height: 15–25 meters at maturity
  • Leaves: Compound, feathery, bright green
  • Flowers: Golden-yellow, fragrant clusters
  • Blooming: June to September (monsoon season)

Medicinal & Cultural Significance

Medicinal Uses

  • Bark used for treating skin diseases and wounds
  • Leaves have anti-inflammatory properties
  • Traditional remedy for digestive disorders
  • Used in treating diabetes in folk medicine

Cultural Importance

  • Popular ornamental tree in parks and gardens
  • Symbol of prosperity and abundance
  • Widely planted along streets for shade
  • Featured in tropical landscape architecture

Economic Uses

  • Wood used for construction and furniture
  • Bark contains tannins for leather processing
  • Honey production from flower nectar
  • Seeds used in traditional crafts

Cultivation & Care

Sunlight

Yellow Flame Trees thrive in full sunlight and require at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily for optimal flowering.

Watering

Moderate watering is sufficient. The tree is drought-tolerant once established but benefits from regular watering during dry spells.

Soil

Adaptable to various soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile soil. Can tolerate slightly alkaline to acidic conditions.

Pruning

Minimal pruning required. Remove dead or damaged branches and shape the canopy during dormant season for better structure.

Interesting Facts

Flowering Season

The tree produces masses of golden-yellow flowers during the monsoon season, creating a spectacular display that can be seen from miles away.

Wildlife Value

Attracts bees, butterflies, and birds. The nectar-rich flowers support pollinators, while the seeds provide food for various bird species.

Growth Rate

Fast-growing tree that can reach significant height within 5-7 years. Develops a broad, umbrella-shaped crown with age.

Climate Tolerance

Thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Can withstand occasional frost but prefers warm, humid conditions year-round.
